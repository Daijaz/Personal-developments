The Life of Steve Jobs

Co-founder of Apple Inc.

Early Life

Steve Jobs was born on February 24, 1955, in San Francisco, California. He was adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs and grew up in Mountain View, California. Jobs attended Reed College in Portland, Oregon, for one semester before dropping out and returning to California.

Career at Apple

In 1976, Jobs and Steve Wozniak co-founded Apple Computer Inc. with the goal of creating personal computers that were affordable and accessible to everyone. Jobs was the face of the company and played a key role in its growth and success. Under his leadership, Apple released many innovative products, including the first Macintosh computer and the iPod, iPhone, and iPad.

Legacy

Steve Jobs passed away on October 5, 2011, after a long battle with pancreatic cancer. Despite his short life, Jobs left a lasting impact on the world and changed the way people think about technology. He will always be remembered as a visionary and creative entrepreneur who helped shape the future of personal computing.
